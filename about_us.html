Organization History

Bronx Family Network Inc. was founded in 2018 by a group of individuals with a shared passion for helping the needy and unfortunate. Amongst these individuals are people with years of experience working with but not limited to, NYC’s Department of Homeless Services helping the homeless; as well as an involvement with other charitable activities. Our staff has deep relationships with diverse communities and is committed to providing support and relief to those who need it most. Bronx Family Network Inc. is affiliated with 731 Southern Boulevard Family Residency, a shelter in the community providing temporary housing for the less fortunate.

Mission and Vision

Our services provide an alternative for homeless families in need of temporary transitive housing. Bronx Family Network Inc. allows for individuals and families to maintain their dignity while affording them the opportunity to regain their independence in a safe and healthy environment. Our services include full showers and kitchens, access to clothing, case management and appropriate referrals to substance abuse treatment and rehabilitation centers, medical and health services along with connections to other services and the support needed to transition to permanent stable housing.

Trained case workers work with our families to identify and refer them to the appropriate community and legal resources needed to obtain benefits, immigration support, and healthcare. Case workers also encourage our families to further their education, language and marketable skills to further enable them to live independently and to contribute to their communities.

Executive Director

Mrs. Mateo has a BA from St. Lawrence University where she majored in Government and Spanish. Shortly after graduating from St. Lawrence University she began to work in the finance world. Throughout her career Mrs. Mateo has been involved as both an organizer and supporter of various community organizations and groups. Mrs. Mateo always knew that her purpose was to help people which is why in 2011 she began her career as a Case Manager. She quickly climbed the ladder and in just two years became a director. With over five years of experience as a Shelter Director and now an Executive Director , Mrs. Mateo can make more of a difference in her community.
